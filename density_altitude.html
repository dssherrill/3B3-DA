Density altitude is computed from actual air density rather than the FAA's dry-air rule of thumb: dewpoint gives the saturation vapor pressure (Tetens formula), which combines with temperature and pressure into a virtual temperature — the temperature dry air would need to match the density of the actual, moist air. Density altitude is then the altitude in the standard atmosphere with that same density. Humid air is less dense than dry air at the same temperature and pressure, so higher humidity pushes density altitude up. Pressure altitude (dry) and field elevation still use the standard atmosphere directly, unaffected by moisture. Data: api.weather.gov (temperature, dewpoint) and open-meteo.com (pressure, elevation). For flight planning, always cross-check current METARs / TAFs and official briefings.
